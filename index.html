LLM serving characterization — performance report

Latency is a distribution, and the tail is where serving breaks.

A reproducible harness that decomposes inference into compute‑bound prefill and memory‑bound decode, then treats per‑request latency as a stochastic process across batching and concurrency regimes — mapping the throughput / latency Pareto frontier and the point where p99 detaches from the mean. And it doesn’t just report the numbers: certify issues a verdict on whether they deserve to be believed.

04

Methodology: why one number lies

Latency is a correlated process, not i.i.d. samples

Consecutive request latencies are coupled through KV‑cache occupancy and scheduler state, so naive averaging understates uncertainty. The harness reports the full distribution, measures the autocorrelation between successive requests, and runs an Allan‑variance‑style convergence check to find the averaging window at which throughput actually stabilizes.

Warmup is detected and discarded automatically rather than by eye, and every point is reported with p50 / p95 / p99 plus a stability flag — so the numbers are honest under load, not just on a quiet GPU.

Coordinated omission, addressed: a closed‑loop worker pool slows its arrivals whenever the server slows down, silently understating the tail. The harness also runs an open‑loop Poisson sweep — arrival times pre‑drawn, seeded, never conditioned on completions — so queueing delay lands in TTFT/ITL where it belongs. And utilization is measured, not asserted: a background sampler logs SM‑busy and memory‑controller‑busy % on the same clock as every request.

05

Certify: should you believe your benchmark?

Every harness will print “p99 = 212 ms.” None will tell you whether that number is a statistic or an anecdote. morpheus certify turns the stats layer into verdicts — and it referees any harness’s raw latencies, not just its own.
